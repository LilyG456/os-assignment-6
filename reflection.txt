1. Stability as hogs increase

As I increased the number of CPU hogs while using taskset, the interactive process remained very stable for most runs. 
I tried 0 up to 50 hogs, and the elapsed time consistently stayed close to 300 ms. \
The jitter also was very low (between 0.06 ms and 0.15 ms).
This shows that the system is able to maintain responsiveness even under significant CPU load.

2. When did instability appear?

It was rare to have instability, when I ran it.
The largest was when I ran 12 hogs, and one click had elapsed = 301.889 ms and jitter = 1.889 ms.

3. Perception

I didn't notice much lag. I think I noticed a bit when the jitter value was greater than 1 ms.

Even when I increased the number of hogs significantly (16, 24, 30, and 50), the system was mostly stable, with the jitter being low and consistent. 

4. Prioritization

Based on what I observed, the Linux CFS appears to prioritize a combination of 
Fairness, ensuring all processes get CPU time, and responsiveness, low latency, for interactive processes.

CFS maintains responsiveness by tracking how much CPU time each process has used and prioritizing processes that have used less CPU time.

In this assignment, the CPU hogs continuously consumed most of the CPU time, 
while the interactive process spent most of its time sleeping.
When the interactive process woke up, it had used very little CPU time, 
so the scheduler gave it priority over the CPU-bound processes.
This allowed the interactive process to run almost immediately,
keeping jitter low even when many CPU hogs were present. 
Occasional spikes in jitter occurred when there was more contention, 
but overall, the responsiveness of the system was maintained.
